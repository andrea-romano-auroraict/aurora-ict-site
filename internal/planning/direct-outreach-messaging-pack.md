# Aurora ICT Direct Outreach Messaging Pack

**Status:** Internal working draft (review before external use)  
**Use case:** First-pass outreach for leadership conversations with growing businesses  
**Tone:** Practical, calm, business-focused, credible, non-pushy

## 1) Outreach Messaging Framework (Short)

### A) Problem-led
Use when a prospect is clearly feeling friction but may not have named the root cause.

- **Pattern:** “We see [business pain] when [operating pattern] is in place.”
- **Focus:** Cost drag, unclear ownership, stalled execution, reactive support loops
- **Close:** Invite a quick comparison against what “good” looks like

### B) Insight-led
Use when the audience is strategic and wants decision clarity, not technical detail.

- **Pattern:** “The issue usually isn’t a lack of effort; it’s a lack of sequencing/governance.”
- **Focus:** Priority clarity, roadmap confidence, better business outcomes from existing spend
- **Close:** Offer a practical lens, checklist, or one-page framework

### C) Soft-offer-led
Use when interest exists but trust is still being built.

- **Pattern:** “If useful, we can run a lightweight baseline review and outline next steps.”
- **Focus:** Low-pressure first step (Technology Health Check / baseline review)
- **Close:** Simple opt-in CTA, no hard sell

## 3) What to Avoid in Outreach Wording

- Generic MSP language: “24/7 support,” “one-stop shop,” “managed everything” without business context
- Hype claims: “transform your business overnight,” “best-in-class everything”
- Fear-heavy security framing that sounds alarmist
- Tool-first messaging before clarifying business outcomes
- Long, technical paragraphs in first contact
- Pushy CTAs: “Can we book 30 minutes this week?” in first-touch outreach

## 4) Strongest CTA Styles for Aurora ICT

Use low-pressure, value-first CTAs that match Aurora ICT’s advisory positioning:

- “If useful, I can share a one-page checklist/framework.”
- “Happy to send a short 90-day prioritisation template.”
- “Open to a brief comparison of your current approach vs a practical baseline?”
- “Would it help if I sent a concise outline of how we scope a Technology Health Check?”

**CTA principle:** Offer clarity first, meeting second.

## 5) Recommended Usage by Scenario

Use this as a quick-pick layer so teams can apply the existing drafts consistently without rewriting from scratch.

### 1) Cold LinkedIn outreach to a business owner / founder
- **Use first:** Draft **1** (Technology Health Check) or **3** (cost/vendor sprawl)
- **Why this fits:** Founders usually respond to decision clarity, cost control, and reduced complexity without feeling pushed into a large project.
- **CTA option:**  
  - Softer: “Open to connect?”  
  - Stronger: “If helpful, I can share a one-page baseline checklist.”

### 2) Cold LinkedIn outreach to an operations leader / COO
- **Use first:** Draft **2** (reactive IT / roadmap clarity)
- **Why this fits:** COOs and operations leaders often care most about sequencing, ownership, and execution rhythm.
- **CTA option:**  
  - Softer: “Happy to connect.”  
  - Stronger: “Happy to share a 90-day sequencing template we use with operations teams.”

### 3) Follow-up after a LinkedIn connection is accepted
- **Use first:** Draft **5** (roadmap clarity) as default, then **4** (security baseline) or **6** (cost/waste) based on profile context.
- **Why this fits:** A light value follow-up builds credibility and keeps outreach consultative rather than sales-led.
- **CTA option:**  
  - Softer: “If useful, I can send the template/checklist.”  
  - Stronger: “Would you like me to send the one-page version?”

### 4) Warm outreach after a referral or introduction
- **Use first:** Draft **1** or **5**, with a referral opener added on line one (e.g., “Referred by [Name]”).
- **Why this fits:** Warm context lowers trust barriers, so clarity-led messaging lands better than generic capability language.
- **CTA option:**  
  - Softer: “Happy to share an outline if useful.”  
  - Stronger: “Would a short baseline outline be useful before we speak?”

### 5) First-pass cold email to a business leader
- **Use first:** Draft **7** for broad business clarity conversations; use **8** when risk/resilience is the visible priority.
- **Why this fits:** Both emails lead with practical business outcomes, then offer a low-pressure next step.
- **CTA option:**  
  - Softer: “I can send a short outline/framework.”  
  - Stronger: “Open to a brief exchange on your current priorities?”

### 6) Follow-up after someone engages with Aurora ICT content
- **Use first:** Draft **4**, **5**, or **6** matched to the topic they engaged with (security, roadmap, or cost/vendor complexity).
- **Why this fits:** Topic-matched follow-up feels relevant and attentive, not automated.
- **CTA option:**  
  - Softer: “If useful, I can share the related one-page checklist.”  
  - Stronger: “Would you like the short version we use in leadership reviews?”
