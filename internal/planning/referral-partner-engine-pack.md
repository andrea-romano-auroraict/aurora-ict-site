# Aurora ICT — Referral/Partner Engine Planning Pack (Internal)

> **Internal planning asset only.**
> Not for public website or external publication.

## 1) Recommended referral partner types

1. **MSPs without strategy depth**
   - Strong at support/managed services but may not offer business-aligned technology planning.
   - Likely to encounter clients needing roadmap, modernization, and decision support.

2. **Web/design/development agencies**
   - Often own digital execution but not broader systems/process strategy.
   - Their clients frequently need help connecting operations, data, and systems.

3. **Software implementers/integrators**
   - Focused on implementation of a platform; clients still need cross-platform strategy.
   - Good source of handoffs when scope goes beyond their product specialty.

4. **Cybersecurity specialists**
   - Strong on security controls and compliance, but clients may also need practical ICT uplift planning.
   - Natural adjacency between risk reduction and operational technology improvements.

5. **Accountants/bookkeepers**
   - Trusted advisor relationship with SMB owners.
   - Visibility into inefficiencies, manual workarounds, and reporting pain.

6. **Business advisors / coaches**
   - See growth barriers and execution gaps.
   - Can refer clients who need tech-enabled process and operational improvement.

7. **Fractional CFOs**
   - Strong fit due to ROI focus, cashflow discipline, and systems/reporting reliability needs.
   - Frequently influence investment priorities and can validate business case.

8. **Operations consultants**
   - Identify process bottlenecks and scaling constraints.
   - Referral pathway when technology and systems become the limiting factor.

9. **Local business connectors** (chambers, industry groups, B2B community leaders)
   - Broad access to business owners.
   - Useful for warm introductions and trust transfer at low cost.

## 2) Highest-priority partner types first (and why)

### Priority Tier 1 (start here)

1. **Fractional CFOs**
   - High trust with decision-makers.
   - Commercially grounded conversations (cost, risk, return).
   - Likely to champion Aurora ICT when business case is clear.

2. **MSPs without strategy depth**
   - Close to day-to-day client issues.
   - Frequent trigger events Aurora ICT can solve (legacy systems, reactive spend, unclear roadmap).
   - Potential for non-competitive collaboration if boundaries are clear.

3. **Business advisors / operations consultants**
   - Already diagnosing strategic and operational pain.
   - Aurora ICT can become their technology execution/strategy partner.

### Priority Tier 2 (build in parallel)

4. **Web/dev agencies**
5. **Software implementers**
6. **Cybersecurity specialists**

- Good complement partners with recurring overlap.
- Require clearer positioning to avoid “scope conflict” concerns.

### Priority Tier 3 (longer-term network)

7. **Accountants/bookkeepers**
8. **Local business connectors**

- Valuable for breadth and steady pipeline.
- Usually lower immediate conversion; best with consistent nurture.

## 3) Referral-friendly explanation of Aurora ICT

### Short version (1–2 lines)

**Aurora ICT helps growing businesses make better technology decisions and implement practical improvements that reduce friction, lower risk, and support growth.**

### Medium version (partner-facing)

**Aurora ICT works with SMEs that have outgrown ad hoc systems and need clearer direction. We assess current technology, identify business-critical gaps, and provide a practical roadmap that aligns systems, process, and priorities. We focus on usable outcomes—not overengineering—and can coordinate with existing providers where needed.**

### “When to refer Aurora ICT” version

Refer Aurora ICT when a client:
- is scaling but systems/processes are becoming a bottleneck;
- has too many disconnected tools or manual handoffs;
- is spending on technology reactively without a clear plan;
- needs a practical technology roadmap before making major investments;
- has recurring operational issues tied to data quality, workflow, or platform fit;
- needs independent guidance that is business-focused (not product-push).

## 5) What Aurora ICT should avoid saying in referral conversations

Avoid language that is:
- **Too broad/vague**: “We do everything in tech.”
- **Tool-centric**: leading with platforms before business outcomes.
- **Overpromising**: “We can fix everything quickly.”
- **Competitive/threatening to partners**: implying replacement of existing providers.
- **Jargon-heavy**: terms partners can’t easily repeat to clients.

Prefer:
- clear scope boundaries;
- business problem framing;
- practical outcomes;
- collaboration-first positioning with existing providers.

## 6) Lightweight first outreach plan (potential referral partners)

### Step 1: Build a focused target list (Week 1)
- Create a list of **20–30 candidates** across Tier 1 partner types.
- Capture: partner name, niche, ideal client profile, network strength, relationship status, next action.

### Step 2: Warm-first outreach (Weeks 1–3)
- Start with existing contacts and mutual connections.
- Use a short message: who Aurora ICT helps, referral triggers, collaboration stance.
- Goal: **15 intro conversations**.

### Step 3: Intro meeting structure (30 mins)
- 10 min: understand partner model, clients, common pain points.
- 10 min: explain Aurora ICT positioning and referral triggers.
- 10 min: agree practical next steps (e.g., 1–2 suitable client intros).

### Step 4: Enablement follow-up
After each meeting, send:
- 3-line Aurora ICT description;
- “when to refer” trigger list;
- copy-paste intro template;
- preferred contact path and response expectation.

### Step 5: Pilot period (first 60 days)
- Focus on quality over volume.
- Track: intros received, fit quality, conversion to discovery call, conversion to engagement.

## 7) Simple follow-up rhythm for referral relationship building

### Ongoing cadence (lightweight)
- **Monthly:** short check-in (email or call) with key partners.
- **Quarterly:** deeper review on referral quality, wins, and missed-fit examples.
- **Ad hoc:** immediate thank-you/update when a referral is made.

### Suggested structure for each check-in
1. Quick update on relevant client outcomes (non-confidential).
2. Reminder of current best-fit referral triggers.
3. Ask: “Who in your current client set might benefit from this now?”
4. Offer reciprocal value (insight, connection, practical resource).

### Relationship hygiene standards
- Respond quickly to referred leads.
- Close the loop with referring partner (status + next step).
- Never bypass/refreeze partner trust boundaries.
- Keep communication concise and reliable.

## 8) Starter partner shortlist guide (first 10 targets)

### What a strong first shortlist should look like
- **Balanced, not random:** a deliberate mix across 3–4 partner types, not 10 of the same profile.
- **Warmth first:** at least half of the list should be warm or warm-adjacent contacts.
- **Commercial relevance:** each target should have regular access to businesses that match Aurora ICT’s ideal client profile.
- **Low-friction collaboration:** include partners where Aurora ICT can complement (not compete with) their core offer.

### How to choose the first 10 targets
1. Start from the Week 1 longlist (20–30 names).
2. Filter out targets with unclear client fit or obvious channel conflict.
3. Score each remaining target using the simple criteria below (1–5 scale).
4. Select the highest-scoring 10, while preserving partner-type mix.
5. Mark 3 as “start immediately” and 7 as “next wave” for sequencing.

### Simple scoring criteria (1 = low, 5 = high)
- **Trust/access to decision-makers**
- **Overlap with Aurora ICT ideal clients**
- **Likelihood of non-competitive collaboration**
- **Ease of warm introduction**

**Simple total score:** add the four scores (max 20).
- **16–20:** priority now
- **12–15:** nurture and test
- **<12:** keep in reserve for later

### Suggested mix for the first 10 targets
Aim for a practical spread:
- **3** fractional CFOs
- **3** MSPs without strategy depth
- **2** business advisors / operations consultants
- **2** from Tier 2 (web/dev agencies, software implementers, or cybersecurity specialists)

This keeps the first batch weighted to likely near-term referrals while still testing one secondary channel.

### Encouraging early signals (first 30–60 days)
Treat these as positive signs that the engine is viable:
- **Conversations:** 6–8 quality partner conversations completed.
- **Introductions:** 3–5 warm client introductions received.
- **Fit quality:** at least half of intros match Aurora ICT’s target profile.
- **Velocity:** referred leads are progressing to discovery calls within 1–2 weeks.
- **Partner behavior:** at least 2 partners proactively ask for reusable intro language or referral criteria.

If these signals are weak, adjust mix and messaging quickly rather than increasing outreach volume.
