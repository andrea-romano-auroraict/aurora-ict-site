# Aurora ICT Channel Priority + Weekly Execution Plan (6–8 Weeks)

**Status:** Internal planning asset (working document)  
**Time horizon:** Next 6–8 weeks  
**Planning intent:** Focus founder time on channels most likely to convert to advisory conversations and Technology Health Checks.

## 1) Recommended channel priorities (next 6–8 weeks)

### Primary channels (majority of effort)
1. **Founder-led LinkedIn**  
   Use the existing repurposing pack to publish practical, insight-led posts and start warm conversations.
2. **Direct outreach (LinkedIn + email)**  
   Use the approved outreach pack to turn ICP lists and engagement into conversations.
3. **Website conversion support (not expansion)**  
   Keep core service pages, Technology Health Check page, and contact pathways current and conversion-ready.

### Secondary channels (supporting effort)
4. **Referral / partner development**  
   Build a small, consistent rhythm with accountants, business advisors, MSP-adjacent providers, and trusted operators.
5. **Local presence**  
   Selective attendance at relevant founder/operations events for relationship depth, not volume.
6. **Narrow Google Ads support**  
   Limited test for high-intent terms around IT consulting/health check, only while tightly budget-capped.

---

## 2) Primary vs secondary effort recommendation

### Recommended effort split
- **Primary (about 70–80% of weekly execution time):**
  - Founder-led LinkedIn
  - Direct outreach
  - Website conversion maintenance
- **Secondary (about 20–30%):**
  - Referral/partner development
  - Local presence
  - Narrow Google Ads support

### Why this split now
- Aurora already has approved core assets (services pages, Technology Health Check offer, Insights, LinkedIn/outreach packs), so the fastest path is **distribution + conversation generation**, not net-new asset production.
- Founder-led trust channels (LinkedIn + direct outreach + warm referrals) are higher-signal and lower-waste for this stage than broad paid acquisition.

---

## 3) Practical weekly execution rhythm (founder-manageable)

## Weekly cadence (example)
- **Monday (60–90 min):**
  - Publish/post #1 (or schedule)
  - Build/refine outreach list for the week
  - Select this week’s outreach angle (cost clarity, roadmap clarity, or security baseline)
- **Tuesday (60 min):**
  - Send first batch of direct outreach
  - Respond to LinkedIn comments/inbound messages
- **Wednesday (45–60 min):**
  - Follow-up wave #1
  - Referral touchpoints (intro asks / reconnects)
- **Thursday (60 min):**
  - Publish/post #2
  - Send second outreach batch
- **Friday (45–60 min):**
  - Follow-up wave #2
  - Light website/content maintenance
  - Weekly review: conversations started, replies, calls booked, lessons for next week

### Execution rules
- Keep each week anchored to **one core narrative** (e.g., reducing IT cost waste, moving from reactive IT to roadmap, practical security baseline).
- Reuse approved content; avoid creating net-new long-form content unless a clear gap appears.
- Prioritize reply speed (same day where possible) over message volume.

---

## 4) Suggested weekly targets (starter range)

These are practical initial targets for a founder-led motion and can be adjusted after 2–3 weeks of data.

- **LinkedIn posting:** **2 posts/week**
  - 1 insight/problem-led post
  - 1 practical checklist/framework post
- **Outreach volume (new first touches):** **25–40/week**
  - Mix of LinkedIn connection messages + targeted emails
- **Follow-ups:** **20–30/week**
  - Prioritize warm responders and recent engagers first
- **Referral activity:** **3–5 touches/week**
  - Intro requests, check-ins, or value-share messages with partners/referrers
- **Website/content maintenance:** **45–60 min/week**
  - Keep CTA clarity, offer language, and internal linking current
  - Minor updates only; no major build cycle

### Simple output KPI set (weekly)
- Conversations started
- Positive reply rate
- Calls booked
- Technology Health Check discussions opened
- Referral conversations initiated

---

## 5) What not to overinvest in yet

- Heavy brand campaigns without direct conversion pathways
- High-budget broad Google Ads before consistent offer-level conversion data exists
- Frequent website redesign work (focus on conversion clarity, not visual overhauls)
- New long-form assets every week (repurpose approved materials first)
- Complex marketing automation workflows at this stage

---

## 6) How channels should work together

- **Insights + service pages** provide credibility and depth.
- **LinkedIn posts** create awareness and start light trust.
- **Direct outreach** converts awareness into specific conversations.
- **Referral/partner touches** improve trust transfer and warm introductions.
- **Website (especially Technology Health Check + contact flow)** captures and converts active interest.
- **Narrow Google Ads** supports only high-intent demand capture while organic + founder-led channels do most of the work.


## 7) First 2 weeks: how to start

### Week 1 (setup + first execution cycle)
- Finalise one ICP list segment (e.g., 25–40 target businesses by role/fit).
- Select one weekly narrative from approved assets (cost clarity, roadmap clarity, or security baseline).
- Publish 2 LinkedIn posts using the repurposing pack (one insight-led, one practical checklist).
- Send first-touch outreach to the week’s target list and run one follow-up wave for responders/engagers.
- Update only critical website conversion elements if needed (CTA clarity, contact friction, link accuracy).

### Week 2 (consistency + optimization)
- Repeat the same cadence with a second target list segment.
- Keep posting and outreach volume steady rather than increasing complexity.
- Run two structured follow-up waves (early week and late week).
- Add 3–5 referral/partner touchpoints (reconnects, intro asks, or value-share notes).
- End week with a 30-minute review: keep, adjust, stop decisions for week 3.

### Metrics to watch first
- Number of meaningful conversations started (not just impressions).
- Positive reply rate on first-touch outreach.
- Follow-up response rate.
- Calls booked / qualified discovery conversations.
- Technology Health Check discussions opened.

### Encouraging early signals
- At least a small but consistent flow of positive replies each week.
- Follow-ups producing additional responses (proves message-market fit is improving).
- LinkedIn comments/DMs referencing your practical frameworks or checklists.
- First referral introductions or partner re-engagement conversations appearing by week 2.

---

## Operating principle
Use the website and insights as proof, LinkedIn and outreach as motion, referrals as acceleration, and paid support as a constrained assist—not the core engine.
